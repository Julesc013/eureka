# Privacy, Redaction, And Copy Policy Review

Future explanation output must be public-safe and bounded.

Required policy:

- Do not expose raw private queries.
- Do not expose private paths, private URLs, local machine fingerprints, IPs,
  account IDs, session IDs, tokens, keys, emails, phone numbers, or credentials.
- Treat source/evidence labels and public text as data; escape output.
- Keep quotes short and necessary. Prefer paraphrase plus source/evidence
  reference labels.
- Preserve uncertainty, conflicts, provisional status, gaps, and not-checked
  scope.
- Do not claim Eureka beats external systems from fixture-backed evals.
- Do not claim source truth, rights clearance, malware safety, dependency
  safety, installability, or production readiness.

User-facing copy should be plain, cautious, and component-labeled. Audit copy
may be more structured but must follow the same redaction and non-overclaim
rules.
